World Language Study and SAT Scores

Students with longer durations of world language study tend to achieve higher SAT scores. This positive correlation applies to both verbal and math sections of the SAT. The most significant benefits are observed for students with four or more years of language study.

Read More
April 15, 2025

Foreign Language Study and College Access

Students studying a world language are more likely to graduate from high school. World language study correlates with a higher likelihood of attending college. Many colleges view world language study favorably in admissions, with some requiring it.

Read More
April 3, 2025

Impact of Language Learning on Executive Function

Learning an additional language demonstrably improves the brain's executive functions. Key enhancements include attention control, working memory, cognitive flexibility, and problem-solving. These cognitive benefits are noted in both children and adults, suggesting a lifelong advantage.
